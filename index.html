Instructions

  • SSD is written for 12 performers of any instrumentation. Each player is assigned one square on the screen and all read from the same screen that is projected in the space. Player 1 is the top-left most square, with Player 2 being the bar to the immediate right of that, and so on for each player like the direction of reading a book. The players can all be placed in one central place on stage, or throughout the concert space.
  • The score follows these rules:
  • The piece is for any instrument of any type. Players are to stay in their square.
  • Players circulate between three distinct actions that appear in their square, they are:
    - SAME
    - SIMILAR
    - DIFFERENT
  • The context of the current action is based on the performed action that preceded it. For example, if a piano plays a Middle C at forte, and an indication of "SAME" appears, then that player does the SAME thing. If a "SIMILAR" action is assigned, that could be something like a G above Middle C at forte, or Middle C at mezzo-piano. While a "DIFFERENT" action could be a chromatic run up and down the piano.
  • Players circulate between SAME/SIMILAR/DIFFERENT. And it is encouraged that all performers meditate on this idea of context within their instrument.
  • As the action assignments circulate, players can carry over actions or continue an action that is in progress until they wish to stop, temporarily ignoring the score and/or more seamlessly blending actions together. For example, if a violinist is sustaining on an open A string, they may keep that action in effect overlapping the next assigned action, even if it is "DIFFERENT", and they can either go into something different, or choose to keep sustaining; as it is a continuous action.
  • Additionally, players may opt for silence during an assigned action, but should only choose this if the context of "DIFFERENT" applies. For example, if a piano player plays Middle C at forte, they can stay silent if the next action indicated is "DIFFERENT". If they choose this path, and the next action is "SAME", then the silence remains.

There is a 20 second pause before the piece begins...
